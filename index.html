MESSAGE FROM THE PATRIARCH'S DESK

It is no surprise that volatility is the new normal. Fast-paced changes, new competitions,macro-economic uncertainties, and technology-enabled disruptions are making the terrain more complex. But in these highly volatile scenarios, as individuals and as an organisation, how should we become more agile and dynamic? I realized that what stalls agility are internal barriers such as unclear decision making, outdated organisational norms, lack of lateral communication, and most importantly risk-averse culture. We have to become more agile as a culture and a company and that means that each one of us has to become more active in our approach. This also means that we need to start wiring this into the processes and how we measure success. We certainly have to experiment more and be okay with making some mistakes. That’s the only way we will be able to innovate faster and better. Also, more familiar we become with the industries, communities, countries, people, and larger ecosystems around us, more likely we are to understand and adapt changes. Some of our traditional, multi-layered decision-making processes also needs to change along with simplifying the processes. We also need to delegate more and encourage our team members to become accountable by giving them more autonomy. I hope to see all of you demonstrating agility as one of the key strengths. Before pausing here, I extend my heartfelt new year wishes to you and your family. May we grow more and learn the most.

KGK PARTICIPATES IN CIIE 2020

KGK participated in the 3rd China International Import Expo (CIIE) scheduled from 5th November to 10 November 2020 in Shanghai, China. Held annually since 2018, the event is the world's first import-themed national-level expo. KGK presented an exquisite collection and was well appreciated by the visitors. The renowned event was personally proposed and announced by Xi Jinping, Honourable President of China.

SODIAM EP STARTS SELLING DIAMONDS & JEWELLERY IN PARTNERSHIP WITH KGK

With the opening of its first store in Luanda, Angola's state-owned diamond company, in partnership with KGK Diamonds, started selling polished diamonds and jewellery directly to consumers. Eugénio Pereira Bravo da Rosa, Chairman of Sodiam's board of directors, informed that the investment manifested Angola's commitment to the sharp development of its diamond sector value chain. Witnessing the great economic potential of the Angola region, KGK has opened the new diamond manufacturing factory in November 2019 and incorporated the latest and highly developed technologies, ensuring the holistic growth of the region.

BMCHRC LAUNCHES AAROGYA DHAM

Bhagwan Mahaveer Cancer Hospital & Research Centre launched Aarogya Dham, the dedicated facility to provide free stay to poverty-stricken cancer patients. The new facility was launched by Chairman Mr. Navrattan Kothari, Senior Vice Chairperson Mrs. Anila Kothari and Managing Trustee Mr. Vimal Chand Surana. While throwing light on the added facility, Mr. Navrattan Kothari informs that the hospital under its objective of lending a hand to the underprivileged provides free treatment to the patients belonging to the weaker strata. The new facility can accommodate nearly 100 patients and has all basic amenities in place. The facility is managed by Cancer Care, a women’s wing of BMCHRC.

BMCHRC CELEBRATES CHRISTMAS WITH CHILD PATIENTS

Bhagwan Mahaveer Cancer Hospital & Research Centre (BMCHRC) conducted an online virtual Christmas party for child cancer patients. The online event was loaded with activities like Dance and Drawing competition. Padma Shree Holder Mrs. Gulabo Sapera graced the occasion and boosted the spirit of cancer patients during the event. The yearly event is conducted under the guidance of Mrs. Anila Kothari, Senior Vice Chairperson, BMCHRC, who also plays a crucial role in uplifting the determination of these children, helping them fight the ailment.
